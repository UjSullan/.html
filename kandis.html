1. Exploring the Internal Profile of Kandis

Kandis (Garcinia morella), also known as Indian gamboge, is a small tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. Its round fruits have a greenish-yellow rind and juicy, acidic pulp. Kandis trees thrive in humid, warm climates and produce fruit seasonally, often in clusters directly from the branches.

2. Dietary Benefits and Nutritional Values

Kandis is rich in vitamin C, organic acids, and antioxidants. Its tangy pulp aids digestion, stimulates appetite, and supports the immune system. Traditional uses include herbal preparations for digestive health and as a natural remedy for fever.

3. Cultural Significance

In Filipino cuisine, kandis is prized for its sourness and is used as a natural souring agent in soups and stews. It features in traditional recipes such as sinigang and as flavoring in local preserves and candies. Kandis trees are often found in home gardens.

Kandis trees are hardy and require minimal inputs, making them ideal for sustainable agroforestry systems. Their cultivation supports biodiversity, soil health, and cultural preservation of traditional fruit varieties in the Philippines.
