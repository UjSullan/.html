1. Exploring the Internal Profile of Jackfruit

Jackfruit (Artocarpus heterophyllus) is the largest tree-borne fruit in the world, native to South and Southeast Asia, including the Philippines. It has a spiky green outer skin and contains numerous yellow bulbs of sweet, fragrant flesh surrounding large seeds. When unripe, it has a meat-like texture and is used in savory dishes.

2. Dietary Benefits and Nutritional Values

Jackfruit is a good source of vitamin C, potassium, dietary fiber, and several antioxidants. It supports immune function, digestion, and heart health. Its seeds are also rich in protein and can be boiled or roasted for added nutrition.

3. Cultural Significance

Known locally as "langka," jackfruit is deeply rooted in Filipino cuisine and tradition. It is often cooked in sweet dishes like ginataang langka or added to halo-halo. The fruit is symbolic of abundance and is frequently grown in backyards across the country.

4. Economic Contributions

Jackfruit is a valuable fruit for local markets and food processing industries. It is increasingly exported for vegan meat alternatives, contributing to income for farmers and entrepreneurs. Its versatility makes it marketable both fresh and processed.

5. Sustainability

Jackfruit trees are hardy and can thrive in diverse climates with minimal inputs. They are low-maintenance, disease-resistant, and contribute to food security. Promoting jackfruit cultivation supports sustainable agriculture and biodiversity conservation.
